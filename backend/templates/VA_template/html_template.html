3.2 Goals

  • Identify and assess vulnerabilities: Conduct a thorough examination of the web application to uncover potential security weaknesses.
  • Exploit and validate findings: Actively exploit vulnerabilities to validate their existence and assess the severity of potential security threats.
  • Provide recommendations for mitigation: Offer actionable recommendations to address identified vulnerabilities and enhance overall web application security.
  • Ensure compliance and resilience: Verify that the web application complies with security standards and regulations, while building resilience against potential cyber threats.

3.3 Approach

The assessment team conducted a comprehensive Penetration Test, leveraging real-world attack techniques to evaluate the security posture of the application. The engagement began with automated scans of the deployed application, followed by meticulous analysis of the results. Automated findings were combined with manual reviews to ensure a thorough identification of potential security vulnerabilities.

In addition, the team performed a detailed assessment of the application architecture and business logic to identify potential design-level weaknesses that could compromise security. This approach ensured that both technical and logical vulnerabilities were examined comprehensively.

To validate the findings, manual exploitation techniques were employed wherever appropriate. The application was assessed within a live environment in accordance with industry best practices and recognized testing frameworks. The team utilized both open-source and commercial tools including Nessus Professional, Kali Linux, Postman, Nmap Scripts, and Burp Suite Professional. Manual testing was conducted extensively to ensure a complete and in-depth evaluation of the application's security posture.

  • Perform broad scans to identify potential areas of exposure and services that may act as entry points.
  • Perform targeted scans to identify specific vulnerabilities.
  • Conduct supplemental research and development activities to support detailed analysis.
  • Identify issues of immediate consequence and recommend remediation actions.
  • Develop long-term strategic recommendations to enhance overall security resilience.

3.5.1 Reassessment Guidelines

A one-time retest is optional. The retest shall be performed on all identified vulnerabilities as a single consolidated exercise. The customer must confirm the completion of remediation for all identified vulnerabilities as part of the initial engagement in order to avail of the optional retest.

Any retest not conducted within {{report.reassessment}} from the date of submission of the initial assessment report will be considered a “Fresh Audit”, for which additional commercial terms shall be provided upon request.

3.5.2 Out of Scope

  • Fixing or remediation of identified vulnerabilities is not part of this engagement.
  • Post-incident analysis and log review assessments are not included in the scope.
  • A complete network configuration review is not part of the audit scope.
  • Other web services running on different ports, except those associated with the scoped application, are excluded.
  • Third-party services and microservices are not part of this assessment.
  • AWS/Azure cloud security assessments (cloud infrastructure review) are not included in the penetration test scope.

3.5.3 Post Assessment Clean-up

Any test accounts created specifically for the purpose of this assessment should be disabled or removed, as appropriate, along with any associated data or content generated during testing activities.
